Goal

Our goal is to detect networks that intercept your connection and therefore degrade your security.
If an interception is detected, we will characterize the middlebox.

How it works

Research has shown that TLS connections are intercepted. However, focuses only on one side of the connection (server or client). Such single viewpoint approaches identify intercepted connections only to a certain degree. For example, server-side approaches rely on heuristics (e.g. mismatch between HTTP User Agent and TLS handshake fingerprint) and client-side approaches are generally browser-based and transmit their results via a possibly insecure connection.

In this project, we eliminate those deficiencies by controlling both client and server. Our server captures the transmitted TLS handhsake and sends it back to the client. With that data the client can immediately detect an interception. The interception is then analysed in detail by our central analysis server.
